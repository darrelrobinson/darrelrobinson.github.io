A visualization of why dummy variables shift the intercept in linear regression

In the BA Research methods course at the Department of Government we introduce students to dummy variables in linear regression. In class we demonstrate mathematically that dummy variables shift the intercept of the regression line by studying the regression line equation, but a visualization of why this is the case can be helpful so that students can see how this happens. In this project I use interactive 3D visualizations to demonstrate this concept.

The figures are built with the rgl package, click here for the documentation.

Webscraping with R - Exploring popular baby names in Sweden

Ever wondered how to scrape a webpage and analyze the data? The internet is full of interesting data and I’m hoping to incorporate these kind of things into my research in the future. In this post I use the Rvest package to extract name data and visualize some trends.

An RShiny app to explore some of the China General Social Survey

I use the CGSS in my dissertation but it’s not a very accessible database. 1) it’s only in Chinese so I am one of the few (the only that I know) non-Chinese scholars using it. 2) it doesn’t have online analysis like other large survey projects so the only way to get an idea of what is in the dataset is to apply for access and start exploring (this is made more difficult because of point 1 if you, like me, don’t speak Chinese). So, I am working on the first data explorer for the CGSS. Coming soon.
